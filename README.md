# Palestinian Cultural Heritage
Palestinian Cultural Heritage Project

Palestinian Cultural Heritage is a digital initiative dedicated to preserving and promoting the rich cultural heritage of Palestine. This project highlights the traditions, folklore, arts, and historical narratives that form an integral part of Palestinian identity.

# Overview

In 2025, a group of young cultural advocates, led by Diea Ahmed Ikhlayel, launched the Turathna website. Supported by Irish Aid and Hebron University, the project aims to document, preserve, and share Palestinian folklore and cultural heritage with both local and global audiences.

The platform serves as a central repository for folklore, traditional crafts, music, oral histories, and other forms of intangible cultural heritage, ensuring they remain accessible for future generations.

# Features

- Digital Archive: Comprehensive documentation of Palestinian folklore, traditions, and cultural practices.

- Multimedia Resources: Includes images, audio, and video materials to enrich the preservation of cultural heritage.

- Community Engagement: Encourages contributions from scholars, cultural practitioners, and the public.

- Educational Resource: Supports research, education, and cultural awareness initiatives.


# Objectives

- Preserve Palestinian cultural heritage in a sustainable digital format.

- Promote awareness of Palestinian traditions locally and internationally.

- Foster community involvement in heritage documentation and storytelling.

- Provide an educational platform for researchers, students, and enthusiasts.
